Being A TV Show Host

Shortly after being a professer, Suzuki became famous by his CBC TV Series, Suzuki on Science. After the shows third season, because of disatisfactation of it's timeslot and low budget, he quit the show and went back to teaching and researching about science. Then a few years later he was hired back to host the CBC TV show Science Magazine. A year later, he became the host of the very first CBC Radio series, Quirks and Quarks. After that, he left his TV show, and radio series, to host The Nature Of Things With David Suzuki on CBC - the longest running documentary series on CBC-TV, still a show.

David Suzuki Foundation

In 1990 David Suzuki created created his David Suzuki Foundation - a non profit organization, which promotes sultions for enviromental problems. Using evidence based research, education and policy analysis they work for conserving and protecting our enviroment in Canada, to help us create a Sustainable Canada. The The David Suzuki Foundation is constently used by Canadians as a Canada's most credible and reliable source for evidence-based enviromental information. They empower people to take action with in their communities for enviromentel challanges which we all face.

The Blue Dot Movement

In 2014 Suzuki created the Blue Dot Tour, with other celebrities, starting the foundation, Blue Dot it publicized how Canada has not put on our charter of rights and freedoms to have the right to a health enveroment - 110 countries already are ahead of us on this. Since 2014, over 110 000 Canadians, across Canada have been standing up for our rights to be recognized, and protected. Because of volunteer efforts, over 170 municipalities passed resolutions in support.
